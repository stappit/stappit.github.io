Wasserman's AoS, Chapter 8, Question 7

Posted on April 11, 2017 by Brian
Tags: wasserman, all of statistics, bootstrap
Category:

This is a solution to Wasserman’s “All of Statistics”, Chapter 8, Question 7. The computational part can be found on GitHub.

Comparing Distributions

Let \(X_1, \dotsc, X_n \sim \text{Uni}(0, \theta)\) be iid and \(\hat \theta_n := \text{max} \{X_i\}_1^n\). For \(n = 50\), we draw 10,000 observations of \(\hat \theta\) and, for one of those observations, create a bootstrap with 10,000 iterations.

The simulated distribution for \(\hat \theta\) is consistent with its theoretical distribution \(F(x) = (\frac{x}{\theta})^n\). It also converges in probability (and therefore in distribution) to \(\theta\) since \(\mathbb P (\theta - \hat \theta_n \ge \epsilon) = (1 - \epsilon)^n \to 0\).

On the other hand, the bootstrap estimator is discrete with most of its mass concentrated at \(\theta_n\). We need to check its asymptotic properties.

Failure of Bootstrapping

In order to be able to estimate properties of the estimator \(\hat \theta_n\) from the bootstrap estimator \(\hat \theta_n^*\), we require that the latter converge in some way to the former. We now show that there is no such convergence.

No Convergence in Probability

When \(X_1, \dotsc, X_n\) are pairwise distinct, the probability that a bootstrap is not the maximum is \(\mathbb P (X_i^* < \theta_n \vert X_1, \dotsc, X_n) = (n-1) / n\). From the definition of \(e\), it follows that \(\mathbb P (\hat \theta_n^* = \hat \theta_n \vert X_1, \dotsc, X_n) = 1 - (\frac{n-1}{n})^n \to 1 - e \ne 1\). The fact that this probability is not 1 implies that \(\widehat \theta_n^*\) does not converge to \(\widehat \theta_n\) in probability.

The probability that any two observations being equal is zero since this event lies on a finite union of hyperplanes in \(\mathbb R^n\) (i.e. it has measure zero). Therefore the probability that any bootstrap is not the maximum is also \(\mathbb P (X_i^* < \theta_n) = (n-1) / n\). Again it follows that \(\mathbb P (\hat \theta_n^* = \hat \theta_n) = 1 - (\frac{n-1}{n})^n \to 1 - e \ne 1\).

No Convergence in Distribution

Consider the fact that \(\mathbb P (\hat \theta_n^* = \hat \theta_n) = 1 - (\frac{n-1}{n})^n \to 1 - e \ne 1\). This also implies that \(\widehat \theta_n^* \not\rightsquigarrow \widehat \theta_n\). This is due to it always having a point mass at \(\widehat \theta_n\), which is \(\theta\) with probability 0. The distribution of \(\widehat \theta_n\) is continuous so has not point mass anywhere.
